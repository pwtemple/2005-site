Illustrated here is a development currently being undertaken at a former farmyard in the village of Blindcrake, near Cockermouth. The village is a most desirable location in North West Cumbria, inside the National Park boundary.
The development comprises three barns for conversion. Two barns on the site have been converted and sold. The early sale of these properties, prior to the commencement of work, enabled the new owners to be involved in the decisions on the internal layout.
The properties each have their own spacious setting, with the development site as a whole enhancing further this attractive rural community.
The third barn under development is a unique three-storey property with the added feature of a gallery over the living room. This building is currently for sale, and will offer an excellent quality of finished work and thoughtful design.
High thermal insulation to walls, floors and ceilings will ensure that heating costs will be kept to a minimum.
